FiT iT

How to improve the online shoe shopping experience

Overview

When it comes to buying shoes online, Incorrect Size Ordered rank first among the main reasons for the product return with a rate between 20% and 35%. That is, one pair of shoes is returned for roughly every three to five sales.

“Fit It” is a shopping in-app sizing tool that helps users to find out the right shoe size across different styles and with AR technology, to see how the shoe model would fit.

The challenge

The goal of this project was to find a solution that can improve the user’s experience when buying shoes online avoiding returns and that can, as a side effect, help to reduce the impact on the company business and on the environment

Users

I decided to focus the research to improve the experience of all users that are used to make purchases online and that, either don’t want to spend time shopping at the brick-and-mortar store or do not have time at all.

_________ Reserch: Competitors Bemchamrk
started my research by analysing how some of the major players in the online shoe sales industry help their user with the measuring issue.
The following are few size guidance of important brands and e-commerce. They all require users to perform extra actions without providing any kind of personalized help.

The main research questions I was looking to answer to are:

  • How do the most popular brands deal and help users to find their right size?
  • How is their return policy (do they charge for it or is it free)?
  • Is it possible to just change the article or a new purchase is needed?
  • Do they have an app?

Asos provides some kind of personalized help, but it turns to be pretty much the same as the other brands as it is not highly accurate.

Something that is provided by all brands is free of charge return policy.
Seems that eCommerce tends to provide a free return as strength of their customer service. But according to Shopify investigation,

"Returns can be a disease — aggressively attacking profit margins, gutting conversion rates, and ultimately threatening the business"

_________ Reserch: Netnography

After answering all research questions that arose about the market, my attention moved toward the user, as I needed to understand if my hypothesis was real.
My first step was to search the web for users’ experiences and opinions regarding the pros and cons of buying shoes online. To do so I conducted a Netnography that led me to the following findings:

The biggest problem is that the product can’t be touch and tried on causing in some cases frustration to the user.

Some users blame themself for choosing the wrong size number when shopping online instead of complaining about the poor and unreliable tools they are provided to know what would be the right shoe size they are about to purchase.

_________ Reserch: Interviews

To build empathy I interviewed 5 users that are used to make purchases online. This allowed me to collect many stories related to the doubtful quality of the product, the convenience of being able to buy and return the product free of charge directly from home, convenience described as an “addiction”.

On the other hand, some users do not enjoy shopping, feeling anxious to go shopping but forced because not sure to find their size online or to know how the shoe model fits.

“I like going shopping but I don’t have much time now, therefore I prefer online shopping as I know that I can return the item for free”

“My decision to buy depends a lot on the return policy. If it is not free of charge chance are I’ll t not buy the product”

“I find my size but sometimes the shoe is not as I thought and once worn I don’t like it anymore, and I return it”

“I hate going shopping at the store, people and chaos annoy me, I prefer buying online, but sometimes it is difficult for me to find the size so I end up buying always the same model”

_________ Define: User personas, Jouney & Scenarios

Thanks to all finding gathered I created two users personas and, to capture feelings, needs and expectations of potential users toward diverse features I created their journey and scenarios

Eva is a young woman, married and with a little daughter. For her, it is very important to take care of her appearance and she enjoys a lot shopping but work and family keep her busy.

Eva's scenario is about her having to buy a pair of heels for an important dinner she has on Saturday. It is late to buy online so she happily goes to her favourite shop. Even if she likes to shopping, she won’t have a lot of time to try many shoes as it is late, the shop is going to close soon and the baby sitter is waiting for her to come back.

Eva's main pain points:

  • Not being able to know through the App if the shoes are comfortable and to see how they would fit with a special outfit
  • Not having enough time to shopping at the brick and mortar store even if she loves shopping
  • when at the shop, having to wait too long to get some help from the shop assistant or to see how the shoes fit because the shop is too busy

Freddy is a busy Professional, who travels a lot on business. He has many interests and, when he is free, he does not want to spend his time doing something that he does not enjoy, like going shopping in a busy store.

Freddy's scenario is about the need of buying shoes before his upcoming business trip. As he is very busy he shops through the App when he has some spare time. Due to the wrong shoe size, Freddy end up returning the shoes and buying a new one at a brick and mortar shop because he cannot wait for a new delivery.

Freddy's main pain points:

  • Not being sure 100% that the shoes he is buying are the correct size
  • The App does not have a practical and personalized way to help to know the right shoe model size
  • Having to spend his precious spare time going at the brick and mortar store because cannot wait for a new delivery

_________ Ideation & prototype

The result of the research confirmed some of my assumptions and provided some findings I wasn’t aware at the beginning of this investigation. By analyzing all data and thanks to the help of some ideation techniques, I finally came up with “Fit it”.
The idea is to provide a practical and handy way to know exactly what is the right size of a shoe model and to see how that model would fit.

I started creating the information architecture , user flow and low-fi concepts for primary use cases to conduct usability tests with the low-fidelity mockups. After getting some positive feedback and suggestions from 4 real users, I was finally able to wireframe my idea and move forward with the design process

_________ Test: Guerrilla testing

With a functioning prototype in hand, I tested “Fit it “ with 5 users I approached
I wanted to observe and measure if users understand the tool, its value and how to complete its basic functions

Overall, their feedback has been positive, they all found it very useful and intuitive. Good feedback also on the instruction and the design, defining it “very aesthetic”. Also, the fact that the App gives instant feedback is something that has been positively evaluated as well as the fact that it helps to avoid to go to physical stores.

_________ Conclusions
  • Even if all feedbacks were positive, I made some changes on the UI to make it "FiT iT" even more intuitive and easy to use.
  • I didn't develop the concept of the 3D feature with the AR technology, so this would be the next focus of my attention
  • Overall the idea has been found very useful and the fact that one user asked me if it was available also for android devices, made me think that it is really something that can make user's life easier
